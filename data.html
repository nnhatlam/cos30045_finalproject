Who Gets Caught on Their Phone While Driving?

Australia issues hundreds of thousands of mobile phone fines every year — but the story behind the numbers reveals striking patterns: certain age groups dominate, AI cameras dwarf police detections, and enforcement intensity varies wildly between states.

Chapter 1

Who Is Getting Fined?

Drivers aged 26–39 receive the most mobile phone fines — nearly 40% of all recorded incidents — followed closely by the 40–64 age cohort. Surprisingly, the youngest drivers (under 17) account for the fewest fines, suggesting that P-plater restrictions or behaviour may differ from mid-career adults.

Insight: The 26–39 and 40–64 age groups together account for approximately 78% of all fines — highlighting that mid-life working-age Australians are the most frequent offenders, not teenagers.

Chapter 2

Beyond the Fine — Arrests & Charges

A fine is the mildest consequence. For repeat or serious offenders, police can lay charges or make an arrest. The data shows that while charges are relatively common across all adult age groups, arrests remain extremely rare — concentrated in the 26–39 cohort.

Arrests & Charges by Age Group

Grouped bars show volume difference — hover for details

Insight: Charges outnumber arrests by ~40× to 1. The 26–39 group faces the highest volume in both categories, reinforcing their status as the most heavily enforced cohort.

Chapter 3

The Rise of Camera Detection

AI-equipped fixed cameras now issue the overwhelming majority of fines in states where they are deployed. NSW alone generated over 147,000 camera-detected fines, dwarfing its police-issued total. States without camera programmes — like NT, SA, and WA — rely entirely on police.

Enforcement Intensity — Arrests & Charges per Jurisdiction
