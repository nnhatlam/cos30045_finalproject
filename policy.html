Ethics and Data Policy

This page explains the ethics framework behind this dashboard and how we handle public enforcement data responsibly.

Our approach focuses on transparency, privacy, and policy relevance so that decisions are informed by evidence, not assumptions.

The Ethics Behind the Numbers: Our Data Philosophy

Every statistic on this dashboard begins as a real-world enforcement event. We believe that if data is used to influence national road safety policy, the process must remain visible and understandable to the public.

Primary source: Australian Road Safety Data Hub - Police Enforcement

Tracking the Trends, Not the Individual

Behind every fine, charge, and arrest is a real driver. Our mandate is to study systemic patterns, not monitor individuals. Data is used in aggregated, de-identified form to protect privacy while preserving policy value.

Acknowledging the Infrastructure Bias

Detection data is shaped by available infrastructure. Jurisdictions with dense AI camera networks naturally capture more offences, while camera-sparse regions rely more on police patrol activity. We surface these blind spots so users can interpret trends responsibly.

Bringing the Black Box into the Light

Granular traffic enforcement details are often buried in static reports. This project turns those records into an interactive, public-facing evidence base so policymakers, media, and citizens can evaluate trade-offs in road safety investment.
