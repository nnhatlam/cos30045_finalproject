Driving Policy through Data

People often assume mobile phone driving offences are mostly caused by teenagers. The data tells a very different story.

To reduce serious road injuries, we need to move beyond assumptions and focus on measurable evidence.

This dashboard opens the enforcement black box, showing who is most affected, where infrastructure is uneven, and how detection methods are changing across Australia.

It is built for policymakers, researchers, media, and the public to explore demographic patterns, jurisdiction differences, and policy-relevant trends in mobile phone offences.

Why this matters

Mobile phone usage while driving remains a persistent safety issue on Australian roads. Many dashboards stop at broad trends, but this project is designed to expose the deeper variables driving offences: age distribution, legal severity, jurisdiction infrastructure, and detection mode differences.
